Who I am.

My name is Andrés Román Saravia. I am a PhD graduate in Computer Science (CS) and Master Degree advanced student in Applied Mathematics at FaMAF-UNC, Argentina. I finished my Master thesis in CS (titled "Tableaux calculus for elementary formulae in Separation Logics") in 2020 (funded by EVC-CIN) and my PhD thesis in CS ("Epistemic Logics based on abilities") in 2024 (funded by CONICET). Both of them under the supervision of Raul Fervari. I am currently working on my Master thesis in Applied Mathematics on Monte Carlo simulations under the supervision of María Cecilia Giménez.

My interests in research include (but are not limited to):

  • Modal Logics, Model theory, Proof theory and Computational complexity.
  • Dynamic Logics, Epistemic Logics, Separation Logics, Knowledge representation and Automated deduction.
  • Artificial Intelligence, Probability and Monte Carlo Simulations.

Member of the Logics, Interaction and Intelligent Systems (LIIS) group, at FaMAF-UNC (Argentina).
